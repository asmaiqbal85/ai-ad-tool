## What you must not forget

1. **Never promise features you haven't built.** A single broken promise to a Pakistani SMB owner spreads through WhatsApp groups and costs you 10 future customers. Match marketing to reality always.

2. **Don't break the live app for Jamal.** He's your first proof point. Protect him.

3. **Build less than you want to.** You have 150 hours over the next 90 days. The plan asks for only 42 hours of building total. The other 108 hours go to sales, content, and customer conversations. The temptation to build new features instead of doing sales will be huge. Resist it.

4. **Customer revenue funds the next feature, not the other way around.** Don't build Meta Ads integration before you have 25 paying customers. Don't hire anyone before you have PKR 150,000 in monthly revenue. Every spend follows revenue, never the other way.

5. **Match your money currencies.** Collect rupees, hold rupees, spend rupees. Only pay foreign costs (Creatomate, OpenAI) when revenue clears them. Don't let currency swings catch you.

6. **The Month 3 number is your reality check.** If you're below PKR 25,000 monthly revenue at Day 90, something in your message or your target customer is wrong — stop and rethink. If you're above PKR 50,000, you've found something real and the rest is execution.

7. **LinkedIn is your most underrated channel.** Pakistani business owners check LinkedIn daily for credibility signals. One well-written case study post per week, for a year, beats a lot of paid ads.

8. **You don't need investors.** Three of the most successful software companies built in our region — Tally, Zoho, Zerodha — never raised outside money. Bootstrapping is a real path, not a fallback. Build with patience; you'll have something worth owning.
